# Reference Product Notes

These references are product-pattern inspiration, not direct copies.

## Map Exploration

- Google Earth / Voyager: guided place stories, rich map context, educational exploration.
- Geocaching: map search, discovery logging, collections, local/global adventure.
- Pokemon GO: location-based activity, map points, collection, events, social raids.

Foobow pattern to borrow: make the map feel alive with nearby and global good-deed opportunities.

## Gamified Wellness

- Finch: daily emotional check-ins, self-care quests, companion progress.
- Forest: symbolic ritual, visual growth, focus streaks, optional real tree impact.

Foobow pattern to borrow: make progress gentle, visual, and emotionally meaningful without punishment.

## Charity / Real Impact

- ShareTheMeal: small donations, transparent campaigns, teams, goals, monthly giving.
- Charity Miles: personal activity connected to sponsor-supported charitable impact.

Foobow pattern to borrow: keep donation flows simple, transparent, and clearly separate from symbolic karma.

## Cleanup / Community Impact

- Litterati: cleanup actions, community challenges, map/data-driven impact.
- Olio: neighborhood sharing, local good, small community actions.

Foobow pattern to borrow: show many small actions accumulating into visible community impact.

## Social Good Networks

- Be My Eyes: global help network, multilingual support, low-friction assistance.
- Nobly-style kindness chains: stories of good deeds and pay-it-forward behavior.

Foobow pattern to borrow: enable people to support each other without turning the product into a noisy social feed.

## Design Implications

- Main surface should be a map, not a generic feed.
- Daily action should take less than one minute.
- Social interactions should be low-drama and safe.
- Rankings should focus on categories, teams, and collective goals rather than who is morally best.
- Donations must be trust-first and transparent.
- The product should support multilingual and cross-cultural usage from the start.
